# Design Direction: Concept 5

The chosen direction is the warm community accountability interface.

## Product Feel

- A study table, not a classroom dashboard.
- Gentle accountability with another person, not guilt-based streak pressure.
- The tutor helps the user form one shareable insight after every session.
- Community and check-ins should feel visible from the main study screen, not buried in settings.

## Visual Language

- Warm paper background with coral, sage, olive, ink, gold, and blue accents.
- Rounded cards are allowed, but should feel practical and intimate rather than decorative.
- Use small avatar/group cues for accountability.
- Avoid purple-heavy gradients, dark-only dashboards, and religious decoration clutter.

## UX Priorities

- Start studying quickly.
- Make the next prompt feel personal and doable.
- After study, ask for one honest note worth sharing.
- Track streaks and goals, but keep the tone kind.
- Support eventual signed-in groups or partner nudges through Convex.
